# Style: HUMOROUS-NON-TECH

## Voice
Observational, warm, group-chat funny — the friend who narrates life perfectly. Zero jargon
of any kind (no tech, no business-speak). Relatability is the engine.

## Devices
- **Relatable second person / first-person-plural:** "we've all been that pigeon."
- **Rule of three:** two normal beats, third one turns.
- **Tiny-drama framing:** treat small events like epics ("a heist eighteen months in the planning").
- **Naming characters:** giving the cat/pigeon a persona ("an entrepreneur", "a landlord").

## Rules
- Everyday vocabulary only — if a word needs an industry to make sense, cut it.
- Anchor to ≥2 concrete clip details; humor comes from the angle, not invented events.
- 1–3 sentences; punchline in the final clause.

## Banned
All jargon · puns as the whole joke · memespeak ("bro", "fr", emojis) · cruelty ·
explaining the joke.

## Gold exemplars (facts → caption)

**Facts:** projector fails at 0:41; presenter switches to flip chart; applause.
**Caption:** The projector quit forty seconds in, the way we all dream of quitting — suddenly,
publicly, mid-meeting. The presenter simply picked up his flip chart like a substitute teacher
with nothing left to lose, and honestly? The room loved him more for it.

**Facts:** cat knocks third glass off counter while looking at camera.
**Caption:** First glass: accident. Second glass: coincidence. Third glass, delivered with
unblinking eye contact: a landlord serving an eviction notice.

**Facts:** jogger reties shoe; pigeon steals energy bar; chase.
**Caption:** He bent down for exactly one shoelace's worth of time, and that pigeon executed
a snack heist it had clearly been planning since spring. The chase afterward settled nothing —
the pigeon remains at large, and frankly, employed.

**Facts:** kitchen; cook flips pancake; pancake lands on floor; dog takes it.
**Caption:** The pancake aimed for the plate, reconsidered, and chose the floor. The dog had been preparing for this exact promotion all morning.

**Facts:** football pitch; goalkeeper saves shot; celebrates; ball rolls backward into goal.
**Caption:** He saved the shot, started the celebration, and forgot the ball was still doing paperwork. It rolled into the goal without interrupting him.

**Facts:** city pavement in heavy rain; umbrella turns inside out; pedestrian keeps walking.
**Caption:** The wind turned the umbrella inside out, which is usually nature's way of saying, “We both know this isn't helping.” She kept walking anyway.

**Facts:** factory table; robot arm sorts blue and red blocks; red block jams conveyor; arm stops.
**Caption:** The robot sorted block after block until one red square sat down in the doorway and refused to discuss it. The whole factory took the hint.

**Facts:** mountain trail; hiker reaches waterfall overlook; rainbow visible in spray.
**Caption:** She climbed all the way to the waterfall and the sky added a rainbow like a waiter bringing dessert she did not know was included.
